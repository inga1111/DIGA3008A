WEEK FIVE-BLOG POST

My goal is to ultimately produce a website in which I can showcase my creativity

and my projects as a multidimensional artist in a way that is aesthetically pleasing

and serves as an extension of my personality. I would like for each discipline that

I have done projects for, to be accessible on my website.

And mostly I would like my portfolio to showcase my process as an artist and developer.

Not just the end result. Which is why I want to be very particular about my design page and

ensure that it shows my thought process and creative process by including wireframes,

style guides and inspiration.

other goal is to utilise this website as a sort of diary that records the process of

creating a website and what it takes to create a website as a beginner at HTML.

This would serve as a guide not just for me but also for anyone who would like to see

the process of website creation for beginners.

How I want users to interact with my website

I want users to be able to interact with my website in an immersive

and visually stimulating way. In a way that is fun to look at.

I would like for them to be able to see my capabilities as an artist and to access my projects.

As for my target audience, I imagine my users to be people who are seeking someone

with the creative skills that I display throughout my website whether to look for

inspiration from, collaboration with or to request commission work from.

As for what is important, I would say the portfolio page and contact pages are

the most important as that is the whole point of the website and

it is important that people who would like to contact me are able to do so.
